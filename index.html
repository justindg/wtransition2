Navigating Change Workshop
What's happening and why?

Our industry and customer needs are changing:

  • Customer needs and expectations are changing at an unprecedented rate
  • The competitive environment is intensifying with fintechs, tech companies and traditional competitors targeting our customers, products and markets

With change a constant we must continue to adapt; this includes ensuring we have the skills and capabilities to support our future, both roles we need more of and those we need less of.

The current changes are part of our long term strategy and thinking to ensure our continued future success and to enable us to meet customer needs and expectations.

As a result of these changes a number of positions will become redundant, and some employees will be impacted. While we will actively look to redeploy as many employees as we can into other roles within CBA, the unfortunate likelihood is that some employees will be leaving us.

We are committed to making the process as easy as it can be, and are providing a new and enhanced career transition program that offers a range of career and personal support services. Leading through such change can be challenging and stressful, and so we are also providing training and support for leaders whose teams are impacted.

Frequently Asked Questions

The following FAQ answers a wide range of questions relating to these changes and redundancy. If you would like clarity on any of the answers, or have a question that isn’t covered here, please ask your manager.

Why are we making these changes?
Why are we making changes?
  • Our industry and customer needs are changing:
    • Customer needs and expectations are changing at an unprecedented rate, for example expectations on the speed and simplicity of an experience and on our responsiveness
    • The competitive environment is intensifying with fintechs, tech companies and traditional competitors targeting our customers, products and markets
  • We should be proud of the hard work we’ve done so far; in the creation and set up of our platform model and ways of working, in driving process simplification and taking opportunities to automate.
  • We’re continuing to invest in new technology – analytics, cloud and robotics to name a few – and through our new ways of working are giving teams the autonomy to innovate and make it easier to get stuff done.
  • We are strong but with change a constant we must continue to adapt; this includes ensuring we have the skills and capabilities to support our future, both roles we need more of and those we need less of.
Why are we making the changes now?
  • We’ve already made a number of changes to the way we work; for example, the implementation of our platform operating model and increased customer focus and productivity in operations.
  • This proposal is part of our long term strategy and thinking to ensure our continued future success and to enable us to meet customer needs and expectations.
How will these changes make a difference?
  • The changes we are proposing will help us to:
    • Further embed our platform model, agile mindset and ways of working.
    • Reduce focus and resourcing on activities that aren’t a priority for our customers or business
    • Increase our pace and agility
Why can’t team structures and compositions stay the same?
  • We have performed well as a bank and our existing teams and processes have enabled this.
  • However, the competitive environment is intensifying every day with fintechs, tech companies and traditional competitors targeting our customers, products and markets.
  • We have to change in order to keep up with the change happening around us.
What are the changes?
What are the changes?
  • Enterprise Services is making some changes to the structure and composition of some teams.
  • As a result a number of positions will become redundant, and some employees will be impacted.
  • While we will endeavour to redeploy as many impacted employees as we can, the unfortunate reality is that some employees will be leaving the business.
  • Your manager will explain what the changes are in your team and how they affect you.
How many roles will be made redundant?
  • We are not disclosing this number.
Why aren’t we disclosing this number?
  • Since those employees impacted will go through a redeployment process and may find an alternative role within CBA, the number of roles becoming redundant does not necessarily equate to the number of employees being retrenched (ie leaving the business), and so is not an accurate gauge of future team size.
How have the redundant roles been chosen?
  • This proposal has been made by leaders across ES in line with our strategy. While we will endeavor to redeploy as many people as possible the unfortunate reality is that a number of roles are no longer required.
  • When looking at the roles required, leaders have taken into consideration natural attrition, the closure of open vacancies and have reduced contractor levels.
Why is this a consultation and not a decision?
  • We’re giving people the opportunity to provide feedback on the changes and how we implement them. This is the right thing to do by our people and in line with both our values and employee agreement.
Are these changes as a result of the AUSTRAC proceedings?
  • No. Commonwealth Bank continuously monitors and reviews its operations. This is standard business practice and as you would expect we continually review and improve our organization to meet the needs of customers into the future.
  • Critical for us is ensuring we have the right skills and capabilities for the future – both those we need more of and those we need less off – and in supporting our people as we continue to transition to new ways of working and thinking.
Is this in response to recent reports about your cyber security team?
  • No. As part of our responsibility to our customers, we keep a close eye on the services and capabilities available in the market place which is why there is a RFP for cyber security services in the market place. This is not about offshoring our cyber security team; it’s about ensuring we have access to the best possible services and capabilities through one of our partners.
Are you offshoring roles?
  • We’ve been open about the fact we partner with a number of local and global IT suppliers and will continue to do so in order to deliver the best outcome for our customers. These changes are about roles that are no longer required; it is not about offshoring.
Will there be retrenchments?
  • While we will endeavour to redeploy as many people as possible, the unfortunate reality is that a number of employees will be leaving CBA.
  • Wherever possible we’ll reduce the impact on our people by taking into consideration natural attrition, the closure of open vacancies and we have actively focused and targeted on reducing our contractor levels as a priority.
Will any of these changes result in locations closing – like the recent Brisbane announcements?
  • No.
Will the company call for voluntary redundancies before it determines any redundancies?
  • No. The changes are about roles, not people, and a fair and equitable process is always followed to determine those positions which are made redundant based on customer and business needs.
Are there target headcount reductions across the business?
  • No. This is about having the right people in the right roles to ensure we are set up effectively to deliver for our customers and business stakeholders.
Is shedding staff just a way to reduce costs?
  • Clearly reducing our staff numbers does reduce costs. But this is not the sole driver of these changes. Simply put, we have to change to set us up for the future.
Is it "last on, first off"?
  • No. The changes we are making to our structure are about ensuring we have the right people in the right roles to support our strategy.
Will there be further changes?
  • Change is a constant for our business. We will continually review processes, functions and roles across every aspect of the ES business to ensure we are well positioned to deliver the best possible outcome for our customers.
Has the Financial Services Union been notified?
  • Yes, we are in ongoing discussions with the FSU.

Your Role as a Leader

Leadership support and advocacy is a critical success factor to change. The team will look to their leader for their opinion and approval of the change before being accepting of the change themselves. You will play an instrumental role in influencing how this change is received and understood, you will be responsible for ensuring that the change messages are effectively communicated to impacted individuals and that they understand why these changes are being made.

Your key role is to lead and support impacted employees through the formal consultation, redundancy and redeployment period.

Your role as a leader:

  • The employee remains part of your team until they either secure an alternate role within the Group or are retrenched.
  • You will guide your team through change in a way that meets the Group’s obligations and reflects the Group’s values.
  • You will reinforce the Group’s key messages.
  • The Group must first make reasonable efforts to redeploy employee, where possible, to an alternate role within the Group with a view to avoiding retrenchment.

How do I prepare for discussions with impacted individuals, their team(s) and the wider team?
  • Informing impacted employees of changes to their role can be difficult thing to do as a leader. How you deliver this information and respond to queries makes a significant impact on how the impacted employees will feel during the process.
    • Spend time reading through this document for key Group messages and your business unit rationale for change to ensure communication to impacted employees, their teams and the wider team are clear and consistent.
    • Ensure you adequately prepare for the meetings and are clear on the key points you need to communicate.
    • Practice the scripts and talking points provided to aid you in your discussion.
    • Carefully read through the FAQs to be able to answer any questions.
How do I support impacted employees?
  • You will play a critical role in supporting the impacted employee through the change process. As this can be an unsettling time for our people consider the following when supporting and guiding them through the process:
    • Ensure you understand the formal consultation, notification and redeployment processes to provide well-considered responses to support to our people.
    • Make yourself available to the impacted employeel. Schedule regular meetings to discuss any questions or concerns they may have and be open to feedback they provide. Discuss interest areas across the Group and share contacts you have in these areas for their networking plan.
    • Ensure the issues and feedback raised by employees are fed through the appropriate channels in a timely manner to enable the Group to genuinely consider them.
    • Utilise the support available to you and ensure the impacted employee is aware of the support available to them.
What are the responsibilities of impacted employees during redeployment?
  • As general rule leaders should try and allow employees to focus fully on their redeployment. This may mean transferring or handover of work. Where there are key work deliverables, these should be identified as agreed between the employee and their manager.
Formal Consultation
What is Formal Consultation?
  • Our obligation, set out in the Enterprise Agreement (Division A Section 22) requires that we discuss upcoming changes and impacts to the employee and the FSU, as well as measures the Group could take to reduce the adverse impact of the changes on the employee, before we implement the change.
  • Consultation is a process to discuss upcoming changes and likely impacts on the individual and allowing them to ask questions and raise any issues for the business to consider prior to implementing the change. The discussions aim to provide an opportunity to influence how the change is implemented.
  • It is about providing consideration of individual circumstances in how we implement a change; it is not about whether the change should proceed or not.
What is my responsibility during Formal Consultation?
  • Formal notification will occur for the roles that are impacted by redundancy, taking into the account of the feedback and the adjustments made arising from the consultation process.
Formal Notification
What is Formal Notification?
  • Formal notification will occur for the roles that are impacted by redundancy, taking into the account of the feedback and the adjustments made arising from the consultation process. The Notification provides the employee with confirmation that their role has been made redundant and marks the commencement of the notice period and redeployment activities.
What is my responsibility during Formal Notification?
  • During Consultation leaders are responsible for leading the Consultation Process which involves:
    • Initial Consultation meeting with Impacted Team OR Impacted Individuals
    • Followed closely by verbal update to the team set the context for changes, outline the consultation process and encourage feedback from employees
    • Schedule a follow up/check-in meeting to reiterate messages, answer questions and encourage feedback
    • Facilitate the provision of sufficient information to affected employees throughout the process to enable them to understand the changes and engage with the Group about the impact on the employee
    • Collate feedback provided by employees and submit via email to ‘ESConsultation’ (ESConsultation@cba.com.au) as soon as practicable
    • Meet with your HRBP to review, consider and address employee feedback
Redeployment
What is Redeployment?
  • Impacted employees will be contacted by a Career Transitions consultant who will provide support in finding and exploring suitable internal opportunities during the redeployment period.
  • When a role is made redundant, our goal is to redeploy the impacted employee to an alternative position within the Group where reasonably practicable.
  • If at the end of the redeployment period if the employee has not secured an alternative position, they will be retrenched and will receive outplacement support from Group’s Career Transitions team.
What is my responsibility during Redeployment?
  • During redeployment, the impacted employee is still under your care.
  • As such leaders are expected to:
    • Have weekly check-ins with impacted employees to address any concerns they may have and to ensure that they are connected to the support available to them e.g. EAP, Career Transitions Program and have the opportunity to provide feedback and raise concerns with you regarding the process and their experience.
    • Ensure the employee is provided sufficient time for redeployment activities during work hours.
    • Make any necessary handover arrangements to allow focus on redeployment.
  • Consider:
    • The flow on effects of the changes that are being made e.g. if they are a leader who will their team report to?
    • Are there key stakeholders that need to be advised?
    • What risks and challenges for your team will the change create (e.g. sensitive systems access)?
    • The impact on team morale and how you will deal with this.
    • Walk the floor. Being visible and available to answer questions, reassure your team during the process of change and to observe and check-in with the team.
