What's happening and why?

As part of ES’ strategy to deliver what matters at high velocity, high availability, low risk and the best value, we are making some changes to the structure and composition of some of our teams.

The changes we are making will help us to:

  • Help us to further embed our operating model and ways of working – moving from project-based delivery to agile, using squads and scrums
  • Reduce focus and resourcing on activities that aren’t a priority for our customers or business
  • Increase our pace and agility

As a result of these changes a number of positions will become redundant, and some employees will be impacted. While we will actively look to redeploy as many employees as we can into other roles within CBA, the unfortunate likelihood is that some employees will be leaving us.

We are committed to making the process as easy as it can be, and are providing a new and enhanced career transition program that offers a range of career and personal support services.

Frequently Asked Questions

The following FAQ answers a wide range of questions relating to these changes and redundancy. If you would like clarity on any of the answers, or have a question that isn’t covered here, please ask your manager.

Why are we making these changes?
Why are we making changes?
  • Our industry and customer needs are changing:
    • Customer needs and expectations are changing at an unprecedented rate, for example expectations on the richness, speed and simplicity of an experience and on our responsiveness.
    • The competitive environment is intensifying every day with fintechs, tech companies and traditional competitors targeting our customers, products and markets.
  • Our response is our strategy to deliver what matters at high velocity, high availability, low risk and the best value.
  • We should be proud of the hard work we’ve done so far; in the creation and set up of our platform model and ways of working, in driving process ownership and simplification and taking opportunities to automate.
  • We’re continuing to invest in new technology – analytics, cloud and robotics to name a few – and through our new ways of working are giving teams the autonomy to innovate and make it easier to get stuff done.
  • We are strong and the future is ours for the taking. But with change a constant we must continue to adapt.
Why are we making the changes now?
  • We’ve already made a number of changes to the way we work; for example, the implementation of our platform operating model and increased customer focus and productivity in operations.
  • The decision to make these changes has not been taken lightly and are part of our long term strategy and thinking to ensure our continued future success and to enable us to meet customer needs and expectations.
How will these changes make a difference?
  • The changes we are making will help us to:
    • Help us to further embed our operating model and ways of working – moving from project-based delivery to agile using squads and scrums.
    • Reduce focus and resourcing on activities that aren’t a priority for our customers or business.
    • Increase our pace and agility
Why can’t team structures and compositions stay the same?
  • We have performed well as a bank and our existing teams and processes have enabled this.
  • However, the competitive environment is intensifying every day with fintechs, tech companies and traditional competitors targeting our customers, products and markets.
  • We have to change in order to keep up with the change happening around us.
What are the changes?
What are the changes?
  • Enterprise Services is making some changes to the structure and composition of some teams.
  • As a result a number of positions will become redundant, and some employees will be impacted.
  • While we will endeavour to redeploy as many impacted employees as we can, the unfortunate reality is that some employees will be leaving the business.
  • Your manager will explain what the changes are in your team and how they affect you.
How many roles will be made redundant?
  • We are not disclosing this number.
Why aren’t we disclosing this number?
  • Since those employees impacted will go through a redeployment process and may find an alternative role within CBA, the number of roles becoming redundant does not necessarily equate to the number of employees being retrenched (ie leaving the business), and so is not an accurate gauge of future team size.
How have the redundant roles been chosen?
  • This proposal has been made by leaders across ES in line with our strategy.
  • The proposal to make some roles redundant is not being made lightly. We are very conscious of the impact this can have on employees whose role has been made redundant, as well as their team-mates and work friends.
Will there be retrenchments?
  • While we will endeavour to redeploy as many people as possible, the unfortunate reality is that a number of employees will be leaving CBA.
  • Wherever possible we’ll reduce the impact on our people by taking into consideration natural attrition, the closure of open vacancies and we have actively focused and targeted on reducing our contractor levels as a priority.
Will any of these changes result in locations closing – like the recent Brisbane announcements?
  • No.
Will the company call for voluntary redundancies before it determines any redundancies?
  • No. The changes are about roles, not people, and a fair and equitable process will be followed to determine those positions which are made redundant based on future business needs.
Are there target headcount reductions across the business?
  • No. This is about having the right people in the right roles to ensure we are set up effectively to deliver for our business stakeholders and our customers.
Is shedding staff just a way to reduce costs?
  • Clearly reducing our staff numbers does reduce costs. But this is not the sole driver of these changes.
  • Simply put, we have to change to set us up for the future.
Is it "last on, first off"?
  • No. The changes we are making to our structure are about ensuring we have the right people in the right roles to support our business objectives.
  • In all cases, a carefully managed selection and appointment process will be applied which is fair and equitable for all staff affected by the changes and ensures staff are treated as individuals and with dignity.
Will there be further changes?
  • Change is a constant for our business.
  • We will continually review processes, functions and roles across every aspect of the ES business to ensure we are well positioned to support our business stakeholders and deliver the best possible outcome for our customers.
Has the Financial Services Union been notified?
  • We are in ongoing discussions with the FSU on our changes and the impacts on CBA members.

Resume and Cover Letters
Having a great resume is critical to securing your next role. More often than not, it is the first chance you have to impress a potential new employer, and it will determine whether or not you land an interview.
With so much competition for roles, employers are often inundated with applications. It is estimated that on average your resume has less than 15 seconds to make an impact.
The good news is it’s not difficult to get to get ahead of the pack. Recent analysis shows that around two-thirds of resumes contain at least one spelling error, which means that just getting the basics right will make a huge difference.
Your resume will usually be accompanied by a covering letter or email. There is also an art to writing these and you should spend time creating a basic template that you can, and should, adapt for each role you apply for.

You have skills and talents that are valuable in the marketplace. Your task is to create a simple, but powerful, narrative – your PVP - that outlines what benefits a company will get by hiring you.
This PVP should then be used throughout your job search – in your CV, your covering letter, in interviews and when networking. You should adapt it for each role you target, so that the person making the decision is in no doubt why they should choose you above the other applicants.

Redundancy, Redeployment, Retrenchment
An employee’s position may be redundant if the work (or a substantial portion of it) being done by the employee is:
  • no longer required by the Group to be done;
  • required to be done at a different location, not within reasonable commuting distance; or
  • restructured so that some of, or all of, the duties are split up between other roles.
If an employee’s position is redundant, it is our goal to provide the employee with an alternative position wherever reasonably practicable. The Group will attempt to find an alternative position for an employee whose role is redundant. This is called the redeployment phase.
If an alternative position cannot be found, the employee will be retrenched and will leave the company following their notice period.
Full details of CBA’s Redundancy, Redeployment and Retrenchment Policy can be found here.
